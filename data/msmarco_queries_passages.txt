QUERY: What are the symptoms of diabetes?
PASSAGE: Diabetes symptoms vary depending on how much your blood sugar is elevated. Some people, especially those with prediabetes or type 2 diabetes, may sometimes experience no symptoms. In type 1 diabetes, symptoms tend to come on quickly and be more severe. Common symptoms include: increased thirst, frequent urination, extreme hunger, unexplained weight loss, presence of ketones in the urine, fatigue, irritability, blurred vision, slow-healing sores, and frequent infections. See your doctor if you notice any possible diabetes symptoms.
ANSWER: Increased thirst, frequent urination, extreme hunger, unexplained weight loss, fatigue, blurred vision, and slow-healing sores.

QUERY: How does photosynthesis work?
PASSAGE: Photosynthesis is the process used by plants, algae, and certain bacteria to harness energy from sunlight and turn it into chemical energy stored in glucose. There are two main stages: the light-dependent reactions and the light-independent reactions (Calvin cycle). In the light-dependent reactions, which take place in the thylakoid membrane, chlorophyll absorbs light energy and converts it to chemical energy in the form of ATP and NADPH. Water molecules are split, releasing oxygen as a byproduct. In the Calvin cycle, which takes place in the stroma, the plant uses the ATP and NADPH to convert carbon dioxide from the air into glucose.
ANSWER: Plants use sunlight, water, and CO2 to produce glucose and oxygen through light-dependent reactions and the Calvin cycle in chloroplasts.

QUERY: What causes earthquakes?
PASSAGE: Earthquakes are caused by the sudden release of energy in the Earth's crust that creates seismic waves. The seismic activity occurs most often along fault lines — boundaries between tectonic plates. The Earth's outer shell, the lithosphere, is divided into several tectonic plates that are always slowly moving. When two plates collide, separate, or slide past each other, stress builds up along the fault. When the stress exceeds the strength of the rocks, it is suddenly released, causing the ground to shake. The point where the earthquake originates underground is called the hypocenter or focus, and the point on the Earth's surface directly above it is the epicenter.
ANSWER: Earthquakes are caused by the sudden release of energy along fault lines where tectonic plates collide, separate, or slide past each other.

QUERY: How do vaccines work?
PASSAGE: Vaccines work by training the immune system to recognize and combat pathogens, either viruses or bacteria. To do this, certain molecules from the pathogen must be introduced into the body to trigger an immune response. These molecules are called antigens. Modern vaccines may contain weakened or inactivated forms of a pathogen, or just a part of it, like a protein. When the immune system encounters these antigens, it produces antibodies and trains memory cells to remember the pathogen. If a vaccinated person is later exposed to the real pathogen, their immune system can quickly produce the right antibodies to fight it off before becoming ill.
ANSWER: Vaccines introduce antigens (weakened/inactivated pathogens or proteins) to train the immune system to produce antibodies and memory cells for future defense.

QUERY: What is machine learning?
PASSAGE: Machine learning is a branch of artificial intelligence (AI) and computer science which focuses on the use of data and algorithms to imitate the way that humans learn, gradually improving accuracy. Machine learning is an important component of the growing field of data science. Through the use of statistical methods, algorithms are trained to make classifications or predictions, and to uncover key insights in data mining projects. These insights subsequently drive decision making within applications and businesses. As big data continues to expand and grow, the market demand for data scientists will increase, requiring them to assist in the identification of the most relevant business questions and the data to answer them.
ANSWER: Machine learning is a branch of AI that uses data and algorithms to enable computers to learn from experience and improve at tasks without explicit programming.

QUERY: What is the difference between RAM and ROM?
PASSAGE: RAM (Random Access Memory) and ROM (Read-Only Memory) are both types of computer memory but serve different purposes. RAM is a form of volatile memory, meaning it requires power to maintain the stored information. It is used to store data that the CPU needs quick access to while a program is running. When you turn off your computer, the data in RAM is lost. ROM, on the other hand, is non-volatile memory. It retains data even when the computer is powered off. ROM contains firmware — the permanent software programmed into the memory that provides instructions for booting up the computer.
ANSWER: RAM is volatile (temporary) memory for active tasks; ROM is non-volatile (permanent) memory storing firmware like boot instructions.

QUERY: How does the internet work?
PASSAGE: The internet is a global network of computers connected to each other through a standardized set of protocols. When you type a web address, your browser sends a request to a Domain Name System (DNS) server, which translates the domain name into an IP address. Your request then travels through a series of routers across the internet to reach the destination server. The server processes your request and sends back the requested data, which travels back to your device in small packets. These packets may travel different routes and are reassembled upon arrival. The main communication protocol used is TCP/IP (Transmission Control Protocol/Internet Protocol).
ANSWER: The internet works through a global network using TCP/IP protocols, DNS servers, and routers to connect devices and transmit data in packets.

QUERY: What are the benefits of exercise?
PASSAGE: Regular physical activity is one of the most important things you can do for your health. Being physically active can improve your brain health, help manage weight, reduce the risk of disease, strengthen bones and muscles, and improve your ability to do everyday activities. Adults who sit less and do any amount of moderate-to-vigorous physical activity gain some health benefits. Only a few lifestyle choices have as large an impact on your health as physical activity. Exercise helps reduce the risk of heart disease, type 2 diabetes, metabolic syndrome, some cancers, and improves mental health by reducing symptoms of depression and anxiety.
ANSWER: Exercise improves brain health, manages weight, reduces risk of heart disease, diabetes, and cancer, strengthens muscles, and boosts mental health.

QUERY: What is blockchain technology?
PASSAGE: A blockchain is a distributed database or ledger that is shared among the nodes of a computer network. As a database, a blockchain stores information electronically in digital format. Blockchains are best known for their crucial role in cryptocurrency systems for maintaining a secure and decentralized record of transactions. The innovation with a blockchain is that it guarantees the fidelity and security of a record of data and generates trust without the need for a trusted third party. The data in any given block cannot be altered retroactively without altering all subsequent blocks, which requires consensus of the network majority.
ANSWER: Blockchain is a distributed, decentralized digital ledger that records transactions across a network in a way that is secure and tamper-resistant.

QUERY: How is climate change affecting the world?
PASSAGE: Climate change refers to long-term shifts in temperatures and weather patterns. While some of these shifts are natural, since the 1800s, human activities — primarily the burning of fossil fuels — have been the main driver of climate change. The effects include rising global temperatures, melting ice caps and glaciers, rising sea levels, more frequent and intense extreme weather events such as hurricanes and droughts, and disruption to ecosystems and biodiversity. These changes threaten food security, water availability, and the habitability of certain regions. Coastal cities face flooding risks, while agricultural yields are declining in many parts of the world.
ANSWER: Climate change is causing rising temperatures, melting ice, sea level rise, extreme weather events, and threats to food security, water, and ecosystems worldwide.
